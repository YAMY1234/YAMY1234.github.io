A Page about Myself

Created2023-12-13|Updated2022-12-14
|Post View:

This is a two minutes video about myself~

Thanks for your time getting to know me o(￣▽￣)ブ!!!

And, if you would like to briefly peek through my 4-year undergraduate life and growth, grades, and ranking in each period? See below~

freshman

course

My freshman year was dominated by general courses and basic computer programming courses. During this time, I took advanced mathematics (also known as calculus), which is notoriously difficult at our school, with less than 15% getting a score higher than 3/5. This is also the reason why my advanced mathematics shows very poorly on the transcript /(ㄒoㄒ)/~~），Physics,Circuit Theory, Physics Lab courses; and computer related courses such as: Linear Algebra, Advanced Language Programming.

Due to my good math scores in the college entrance examination, I was eligible to apply for the High-level Language Programming Honorable Course. The High-level Language Programming Honorable Course is divided into two stages: Stage 1 and Stage 2. The first stage mainly studies process-oriented programming skills; the second stage mainly studies Object Oriented programming skills. During two semesters, I gradually adapted to the difficulty. I continued to be handy, breaking through difficulties, and my grades continued to improve, from 3/5 in the first stage at the beginning to 5/5 in the second stage of the practical course assessment.

My freshman grade is not in a good expectation, with the year average GPA of 4.19/5.00 (84.86/100). ranking 182/860 (21%) in Electronic and Information Engineering Institute.

Research training and academic programs

In addition to learning, I also love to expand my abilities in different aspects during my freshman year. I participated in the “Practice Cup Research Contest” of Tongji University, researching on the construction status of Greenland (Lvdi) Financial Center Park in Chengdu based on Gauss two-step mobile search method, and the Innovation and Entrepreneurship Project of Tongji University– the space-time trajectory prediction of autonomous public transport.

In these two competitions, I mastered the basic methods of reading literature and writing academic papers. I also mastered some basic mathematical modeling knowledge, such as proficiency in using Matlab, ArcGis; understanding Genetic Algorithm , Annealing Algorithm, etc. These knowledge and abilities also laid the foundation for subsequent scientific research and learning ability cultivation.

During Advanced Language Programming courses, I also completed many challenging projects, including:

(1) Hanoi Tower Realization - Various Tower of Hanoi variants, training Recursion, Recursion, Recursion~

(2) Minesweeper game - Recursion + desktop window game UI design

(3) Image compression program - Huffman tree construction, for my freshman year is a nightmare

(4) Snake Game - Deep understanding of Object Oriented principles and construction methods

(5) Synthetic Ten Games + AI Operations - Use A Star Search Algorithm to search deep into Recursion

Summer Internship

During the summer vacation, I was fortunate enough to join the internship of Zhonghui. I participated in a 2-month java back-end development internship here. Since I didn’t know much as a freshman, in these two months, I roughly mastered the basic architecture of java back-end and realized the huge gap between me and a professional programmer.

Sophomore:

course

From the sophomore year, I entered the core courses related to computer majors, and initially learned some information security knowledge. I got the highest grades in all most core computer courses: Data Structures (5/5, 95/100), Data Structures (5/5, 95/100), Principles of Computer Components (5/5, 95/100), Algorithm Design and Analysis (5/5, 95/100), Digital Logic (5/5, 95/100), Discrete Mathematics *(4/5, 85/100), Probability and Statistics (5/5, 95/100), Information Security Mathematics (5/5, 95/100), Information Security Engineering (5/5, 95/100), Web Technology (5/5, 95/100).

At the same time, I also took some challenging courses, i.e., artificial intelligence (4/5, 85/100), software engineering (4/5, 85/100). Although I did not get the highest level, I gained a solid understanding of software engineering methods, Machine Learning , and Deep learning theories.

In the sophomore year, my average GPA was 4.73 (91.15/100), and among the 56 students, I ranked 3rd in my major.

competition

During my sophomore year, I participated in many competitions, including various fields, and also won outstanding awards:

  1. Mathematical modeling related competitions:

    I participated in the Mathematical Modeling Competition for US College Students (won the M Award), the Mathematical Modeling Competition of Tongji University (first prize, top 3%), and the Fir Cup Mathematical Modeling Competition (Tongji University, National Defense Science Park University, Shanghai Jiao Tong University, the first place in the joint examination of the three universities).

  2. Computer related competitions:

    I participated in the artificial intelligence challenge and won the first prize in Shanghai; I participated in the National College Student Computer Design Competition and won the third prize.

  3. Information security related competitions:

    I participated in the Tongji University CTF competition and won the Excellent Winner prize.

scientific research

During my sophomore year, I participated in the cross-domain pedestrian re-identification experiment, a national innovation and entrepreneurship project with 4 other colleagues majoring in computer science. This was the first real scientific research project I participated in during my college years. Here I personally participated in the whole process of complete scientific research. Although our innovations were not enough and the excellent results of the experiments did not support us to publish a paper, we all received good rewards and exercises. For details, you can click here to view the content of the Pedestrian Enrichment Research Training Program~

Academic Project

During my sophomore year, I participated in a variety of academic projects, some from courses and some from competitions. Due to my outstanding course project performance, I received the highest evaluation grades for most courses and learned a very solid foundation of computer science.

In the Digital Logic course, I led another member of our team to complete the [CARBuddy design](https://github.com/YAMY1234/carset-fpga# intelligent-vehicle-system-carbuddy-design) project. We developed with limited hardware resources on Nexy4-FPGA board to build a vehicle auxiliary system, with functionalities of driving record, color temperature sensing, music playing, etc.

I designed a multi-cycle CPU that runs successfully on FPGA board within over 100,000 lines 54 mips assembly language instructions.

Enterprise cooperation project employment experience

Besides，I was hired by the “Smart Vehicle” project jointly organized by Tongji University and Dexin Technology Co., LTD,. The project’s goal was an optimal vehicle dispatch planning system for taxi lines. As a result, our system achieved improved function and stable performance and extended to Chongqing, Dalian, and Qingdao with hundreds of orders every day. I also got a monthly income of 3,000 RMB for 14 months~

In this program，I received expertise in the whole process of software development, including requirement analysis, programming, debugging, system testing, operation, and maintenance during this one-year project. Moreover, I learned the significance of realizing software modulization compatibility with rigorous scrutiny to tackle constantly changing demands.

Junior

course

The third-year curriculum also contains many core courses for computer majors, and I achieved excellent performance, including: Operating System (5/5, 95/100), Compilation Principles (5/5, 95/100), Database System Principles (5/5, 95/100), Database System Principles Course Design (5/5, 95/100), Computer Network (4/5, 85/100), Computer Network Course Design (5/5, 95/100). At the same time, I also learned more information security professional courses, and I have won ALL core information security professional courses the highest grades, i.e., Security Architecture (5/5, 95/100), System Security Evaluation and Application Safety Labrotary (5/5, 95/100), Information Security Comprehensive Laboratory Project (5/5, 95/100), Curriculum Design: Principles For information Security (5/5, 95/100), Cyber Security Comprehensive Management and Penetration Testing Project (5/5, 95/100) .

My junior year GPA was 4.86/5.00 (94.30/100), and achieved the 2021-2022 Tongji University first-class scholarship (Ranking Top 5% in major) and Tongji University outstanding student as the second in the class.

Academic Projects and Competitions:

In addition to course study, I actively participate in academic projects and competitions, the main projects are as follows:

Ji Xiao’an - Network Intrusion Monitoring Expert System

We Designed the system as a compatible plug-in module to be embedded in an IoT network to provide security guarantee. Our team as awarded first prize in the Shanghai Computer Design Competition and second prize in the National College Students Network Security Competition

Linux Personal Firewall

I used netfilter and iptable to complete the design of linux personal firewall rules, and adopted PyQT framework to complete the front-end application interface and user interaction

JiYun Resource Management System

I designed the resource manage system based on strict 3-NF paradigm, with functionalities of managing students and teachers’ courseware, homework, grades, according to different user permissions

L2TP-ipsec Experiment

I completed the establishment of a L2TP VPN with additional functionalities, e.g., data capture, protocol analysis, and static load replacement

C-class Language Compiler

I designed a C-like compiler that realized the whole process of lexical analysis, syntax analysis, intermediate code generation and assembly language generation.

Scientific Research

In order to lead my own research, I participated in a security related research, Network Intrusion Detection (NID), at Tsinghua Network Research Lab for three months, and then continued my research at Tongji University. NID detects network assaults to protect networks.

There is also an unhappy experience for my transition. The third month ended with the cancellation of the project. Without any warning, all lab employees were dismissed, and none were even paid or provided with proof of participation. After three months of hard work on this research project, it was not easy to accept this outcome. Without giving up, I contacted professors at Tongji University and carried out experiment on our own. Finally, our results were approved by the CIKM 2022 (ACM International Conference on Information and Knowledge Management), and I was honored as the first author.

Now and Future

Since this summer, I have been currently working as a Research and Development Engineer Intern at ByteDance, Tiktok’s parent company. I participated in the construction of the visual conference quality and security monitoring platform. Specifically, my work involves how to automatically evaluate traditional video metrics, such as delay, noise, etc., using advanced algorithms, and to deal with transmission, storage and maintenance security of sensitive data. During my internship, I developed a video automated evaluation platform with 108 automated visual pathways for Lark Suite and Rooms and 128 video quality and security evaluation indicators. Fortunately, my platform was integrated into bytest.bytedance.net, ByteDance’s internal product evaluation website that receives daily video quality and security evaluation requests from Tencent, Zoom, and many other companies. This internship provided invaluable insights into the industry and motivated me to leverage cutting-edge technologies to resolve real-world challenges.

My short-term career plan is to become an leading engineer in a large enterprise to develop innovative products. In the long term, I plan to set up a team and create a technology company providing security products that are compatible with existing enterprise architecture services. I hope to have more professional project experiences through the master program. real-world application skills as well as cutting-edge knowledge in my field.
